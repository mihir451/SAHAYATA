Before a Disaster

  • Make an Emergency Kit (water, food, medicines, flashlight, documents).
  • Create a Family Plan with safe exits and meeting points.
  • Stay Informed with weather updates and government alerts.
  • Secure your home (wiring, gas, fire extinguisher).
  • Learn basic First Aid skills.

During a Disaster

  • Stay Calm and avoid panic.
  • Follow Official Instructions, not rumors.
  • Move to Safe Areas (higher ground, open space, shelters).
  • Help children, elderly, and differently-abled persons.

After a Disaster

  • Check for injuries and give First Aid.
  • Avoid Hazards like live wires and gas leaks.
  • Boil or filter water before drinking.
  • Report damages or missing persons to authorities.

Flood Safety

  • Move to higher ground immediately.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.
  • Disconnect electrical appliances to prevent shock.
  • Store clean drinking water in advance.
  • After floods, avoid contaminated water sources.

Earthquake Safety

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold under sturdy furniture.
  • Stay away from windows, mirrors, and heavy objects.
  • If outside, move to an open area away from buildings.
  • Do not use elevators during or after the quake.
  • Check for injuries and structural damages afterward.

Cyclone Safety

  • Secure windows, doors, and loose objects outside.
  • Keep a battery-powered radio for updates.
  • Evacuate if advised by authorities.
  • Avoid coastal areas until authorities declare safety.
  • Stay indoors and away from glass windows during the storm.

Fire Safety

  • Install smoke alarms and fire extinguishers at home.
  • Never use elevators during a fire; use stairs.
  • Stop, Drop, and Roll if clothes catch fire.
  • Crawl low under smoke to exit safely.
  • Call emergency services immediately after escaping.
